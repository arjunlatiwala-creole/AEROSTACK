# RevOps Productivity — Feedback & Intent (call 2026-06-29, Will + Paige)

Source: working call transcript + Will's origination comment. This is the authoritative
intent for restructuring the RevOps Productivity module. Captured verbatim-in-spirit;
rephrased for compliance and clarity.

## The core reframe
The RevOps "productivity" screen is the **operating dashboard for the weekly RevOps meeting**.
Its job is to stop people pointing at data as the *source of confusion* and make the data the
*source of clarity*. It must let Will + Paige run the business at a glance without getting
dragged into individual deal/customer arguments.

## Who runs it (universal operators)
**Kyle, Will, and Paige run the business universally.** All three operate across every section of
this dashboard — Customer Defects & Risks, Collections, Opportunities, Transitions, and SalesOps —
with full cross-cutting visibility. The dashboard is designed for all of us and them to prioritize
business choices tied to invest/revenue-generation activity together from one surface.
(This is an operating-intent statement; system roles/access are handled separately.)

> "Break this productivity screen into the things we said: opportunities, in-flight collections
> of invoicing activity, aging of invoices and FRs, process and sales-ops updates, and customer
> issues. Then we talk about each one of those areas in a meeting based on this dashboard."

## The five sections (top-level structure of the productivity dashboard)

1. **Customer Defects & Risks (CoE)** — *right up front.*
   Any exceptions or anything scored bad from the sections below, or from anywhere else in the
   business. This is the escalation/early-warning band at the top of the page.

2. **Collections** — all invoice aging, health, and activity.
   Includes customer, partner, and **AWS FR** invoicing; status, urgency, blockers.
   Two-sided ledger thinking: what we expected to collect vs what we actually collected.

3. **Transitions** — conversion/contracts, handoff/onboarding, 60%–100% post-opp project closes
   and opportunity windows, managed/offboarding. (Identify any other transition types.)

4. **Opportunities** — HubSpot status & staging.
   Aging and blockers, at-a-glance — explicitly so we do NOT have to open individual opp details
   in the call. Surface what matters; keep us out of the weeds.

5. **SalesOps Mechanisms & Process Improvement** — process + sales-ops updates, recurring
   improvements that come out of running the meeting (this doc is an example output).

## Hard rules / decisions from the call
- **Ownerless deals are not a person's job.** Assign all unowned / PC3 deals to a generic owner
  and route them into an automated pipeline (digital campaigning and scheduling of first calls;
  process/requirements owned by S. Akash). Emailing those customers is not anyone's manual job.
  Only real, owned, active deals get human owners (Max, Kyle, Will, NextPerson).
- **Aging:** anything not touched in **>14 days** flags (red). Every deal **or invoice** is either
  managed by a process/automation or by a named owner — no orphans. **First project invoices and
  AWS CST day-0 requirements are immediate** (issued at day 0 and BLOCKING until paid, not aged in).
- **TCV vs realized:** show **Total Contract Value vs Billed/Paid** (realized vs contract) as a
  two-sided ledger. Must roll up at deal → customer → practice → company → geography.
- **RevRec reconciliation is NOT this screen.** A pipeline/productivity view is not where we
  reconcile revenue recognition. Full RevRec = QuickBooks RevRec module (build-vs-buy → **buy**,
  backlog item, needs license investment). For now, visualize contract-vs-realized from data we
  already have; do not build a SaaS accounting system inside Aerostack.
- **No sales quotas foreseeably.** Reps will be targeted and measured on **MBO outcome targets**
  (which may contain revenue-generation targets) **+ OKRs** (pending visible correction) as their
  owed and visible work performed + outcomes delivered. There will be no traditional "sales" org here.
- **Billing triggers come from deliverables/milestones** (project status updates) or from time
  (recurring schedules). The SoW contract holds the invoicing schedule; surface and **automate that
  translation to invoice and signing events** so no one — or Paige — has to dig through the SoW or
  chase the team for updates to know what to invoice.
- **Expectation-setting:** SoW deliverables, invoicing, and financials are set and communicated
  clearly and consistently — supporting "first invoice issued" and a kickoff/move-to-engagement
  together-to-be-successful message. Don't leave invoicing or customer questions squishy for days.
- **Move past Creole on integration.** Integration help (HubSpot→QuickBooks sync) is not arriving
  from Creole; deliver task-based support in Aerostack regardless — do not block module work on it.
- **Sequence-number nomenclature (001/002…)** is accepted for the org but is not load-bearing in
  our data; don't design around it — but try to make it more useful and codified consistently in
  this SoW effort.
- **Don't get dragged into individual deals** in the dashboard/meeting. Meetings get dragged into
  individual deals or one-off, less-valuable questions when they lack the dashboard/meeting
  structure — the dashboard exists so individual-deal firefighting is the **exception**, and
  individual-deal discussion happens **by escalation** as needed to keep deals healthy or moving.

## What to do with it
- Restructure the RevOps Productivity dashboard into the five sections above (CoE band on top),
  ordered: Customer Defects & Risks → Collections → Transitions → Opportunities → SalesOps.
- Collections: invoice aging/health across customer + partner + AWS FR, with urgency/blocker flags.
- Transitions: contract→onboarding→post-close→managed/offboarding lifecycle.
- Opportunities: HubSpot staging + aging + blockers, at-a-glance only.
- SalesOps: process improvements + ops mechanisms log.
- Add TCV-vs-realized roll-ups (deal/customer/practice/company/geo) sourced from current data;
  RevRec module stays a documented backlog item, not built now.
